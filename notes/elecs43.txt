### **1. Knowledge (Recall & Facts)**

**Definitions:**

* **Equivalent Circuit:** Linear network of elements (resistors, batteries, switches) modeling non-linear device behavior.
* **Ideal Model:** Diode as a perfect switch. Zero voltage drop (forward); infinite resistance (reverse).
* **Simplified Model (Constant Voltage Drop):** Diode as an ideal switch plus a fixed opposing DC battery representing the barrier potential.
* **Piecewise-Linear Model:** Diode as an ideal switch, fixed battery, and series bulk resistance representing semiconductor material opposition.
* **Static Resistance ($R_D$):** DC resistance at a specific quiescent operating point (Q-point).
* **Dynamic Resistance ($r_d$):** AC small-signal resistance tangent to the operating point.
* **Average AC Resistance ($r_{av}$):** Effective resistance over a large signal swing between two limits.
* **Knee Voltage ($V_K$):** Barrier potential threshold where forward conduction rapidly increases.

**Critical Numbers:**

* **Silicon (Si) $V_K$:** ~0.7V
* **Germanium (Ge) $V_K$:** ~0.3V
* **Gallium Arsenide (GaAs) $V_K$:** ~1.2V
* **Thermal Voltage ($V_T$):** ~26mV (at 300K / room temperature)
* **Typical Bulk Resistance ($r_B$ or $r_{av}$):** $< 10\Omega$ to $50\Omega$ (varies by diode size/doping)
* **Ideal Model Thresholds:** $V_F = 0V$, $I_R = 0A$

**Standards & Protocols:**

* **SPICE Diode Model:** Industry-standard simulation utilizing equivalent parameters: **IS** (Saturation Current), **N** (Emission Coefficient), **RS** (Ohmic Resistance), **CJO** (Junction Capacitance).
* **JEDEC EIA-370:** Discrete semiconductor numbering (e.g., **1N**4148 implies 1 junction, testing specs map directly to piecewise parameters).

### **2. Comprehension (Concepts & Relationships)**

**Visual Interpretation:**

* **Ideal V-I Curve:** Strict right angle. Vertical line along +Y axis ($V_D = 0V$), horizontal along -X axis ($I_D = 0A$).
* **Simplified V-I Curve:** Right angle shifted to the right. Vertical line begins exactly at $X = V_K$ (0.7V).
* **Piecewise-Linear Curve:** Slanted line starting at $X = V_K$. Slope mathematically equals **$1/r_{av}$**.
* **Equivalent Circuit Diagrams:**
* **Ideal:** Switch only.
* **Simplified:** Switch + Battery ($0.7V$ opposing current flow).
* **Piecewise:** Switch + Battery ($0.7V$) + Resistor ($r_{av}$).



**Pros vs. Cons:**

| Diode Model | Pros | Cons |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Ideal** | Ultra-fast analysis; great for high-voltage systems | Highly inaccurate for low-voltage/precision circuits |
| **Simplified** | Standard engineering baseline; balances speed/accuracy | Ignores $I_D$-dependent voltage variations |
| **Piecewise-Linear** | Highest analytical accuracy for practical circuits | Computationally heavy; requires datasheets for $r_{av}$ |
| **Small-Signal (AC)** | Accurately predicts RF/Audio dynamic behavior | Only valid for microscopic signal swings around Q-point |

**Exam Traps:**

* **Polarity of Equivalent Battery:** Placing the 0.7V battery model opposing the forward current. It is a *drop*, not a source.
* **AC vs. DC Resistance:** Using static $V_D/I_D$ when the question asks for dynamic resistance ($26mV/I_D$).
* **State Assumption Errors:** blindly applying the 0.7V model without first verifying via Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL) that the source actually exceeds 0.7V.
* **High Voltage Irrelevance:** Subtracting 0.7V in a 10,000V circuit. (Board exams often expect the **ideal model** when $V_{source} > 100 \times V_K$).

**Bottom-Line Summary:**
Diode equivalent circuits bridge the critical gap between complex, non-linear semiconductor physics and practical linear circuit analysis by substituting mathematically rigid idealizations—ranging from perfect switches to piecewise-linear resistive networks. By deliberately selectively ignoring negligible parameters based on the system's operating voltage range, engineers can bypass transcendental Shockley equations to rapidly and accurately predict both massive DC operating points and microscopic AC small-signal behaviors using fundamental Kirchhoff laws.

### **3. Application (Calculations & Engineering)**

**Formulas:**

* **DC/Static Resistance:**

$$R_D = \frac{V_D}{I_D}$$


* $V_D$: Diode forward voltage at Q-point (V)
* $I_D$: Diode forward current at Q-point (A)
* *Meaning:* Absolute resistance at a single, unchanging DC operating point.


* **AC/Dynamic Resistance (Approximate):**

$$r_d = \frac{26\text{mV}}{I_D}$$


* $26\text{mV}$: Thermal voltage equivalent at room temp
* $I_D$: Quiescent DC current establishing the Q-point (A)
* *Meaning:* Instantaneous resistance to alternating current superimposed on a DC bias.


* **Piecewise-Linear Diode Voltage:**

$$V_D = V_K + (I_D \cdot r_{av})$$


* $V_K$: Knee voltage (0.7V for Si)
* $I_D$: Forward current (A)
* $r_{av}$: Average bulk resistance ($\Omega$)
* *Meaning:* Exact voltage drop accounting for the internal semiconductor bulk resistance.


* **Average AC Resistance:**

$$r_{av} = \frac{\Delta V_d}{\Delta I_d}$$


* $\Delta V_d$, $\Delta I_d$: Point-to-point boundary limits on the characteristic curve.



**Practical Use:**

* **Ideal Model:** Power transmission grids, AC mains rectification (e.g., 220V bridge rectifiers where 0.7V is mathematically negligible).
* **Simplified Model:** Standard 5V/12V TTL logic, basic discrete power supplies, LED biasing networks.
* **Piecewise Model:** Precision analog computational circuits, low-voltage battery chargers, thermal tracking sensors.
* **AC Equivalent Model:** RF mixing, variable attenuators, amplitude modulation (AM) detectors.

**Design Trade-Offs:**

* **Complexity vs. Tolerance:** Using a piecewise-linear model for a 12V relay driver is a waste of engineering time; standard resistor tolerances ($\pm 5\%$) introduce more error than ignoring $r_{av}$.
* **Small-Signal Limit:** $r_d$ calculations are strictly invalid if the AC peak-to-peak swing exceeds ~10% of the DC Q-point current; massive distortion occurs due to severe non-linearity.

**High-Yield Exam Triggers:**

| Keyword / Visual Cue | Memory Shortcut / Translation | Typical Exam Question Format |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **"Ideal Diode"** | $V_D = 0V$ (Short) or Open | Find current; simple Ohm's Law $I = V_{source}/R$. |
| **"Silicon Diode" (unspecified)** | Assume Simplified Model ($0.7V$) | Calculate $I_D = (V_{source} - 0.7V) / R$. |
| **"Calculate Dynamic Resistance"** | $r_d = 26mV / I_D$ | Find DC $I_D$ first, then divide $26mV$ by that current. |
| **"Bulk Resistance given"** | Use Piecewise Model | Calculate total $V_D$ including the $I_D \cdot r_{av}$ drop. |
| **$V_{source} < 0.7V$** | Diode is OFF | State is Open Circuit; $I_D = 0A$, $V_D = V_{source}$. |
| **"Calculate AC voltage gain"** | Include $r_d$ in amplifier load | Use small-signal diode resistance in parallel/series equations. |
