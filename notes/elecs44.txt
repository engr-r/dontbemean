### **1. Knowledge (Recall & Facts)**

**Definitions:**

* **Voltage Multiplier:** Diode-capacitor network generating a DC output voltage that is a multiple of the peak AC input voltage.
* **Half-Wave Rectifier (HWR):** Converts one polarity of AC to pulsating DC.
* **Full-Wave Rectifier (FWR):** Converts both polarities of AC to pulsating DC.
* **Ripple Factor ($\gamma$ or $r$):** Ratio of the RMS AC component to the DC component in the output.
* **Peak Inverse Voltage (PIV):** Maximum reverse-bias voltage a diode must withstand without breakdown.
* **Linear Regulator:** Dissipates excess power as heat to maintain constant DC output.
* **Switched-Mode Power Supply (SMPS):** Rapidly switches transistors on/off, using inductors/capacitors to regulate voltage efficiently.
* **Load Regulation:** Ability of a power supply to maintain constant voltage despite varying load current.
* **Line Regulation:** Ability of a power supply to maintain constant voltage despite varying input (mains) voltage.
* **Low Dropout (LDO):** Linear regulator requiring minimal differential between input and output voltages.

**Critical Numbers:**

* **HWR Efficiency Limit:** 40.6%
* **FWR Efficiency Limit:** 81.2%
* **HWR Ripple Factor:** 1.21 (121%)
* **FWR Ripple Factor:** 0.48 (48%)
* **Ripple Frequency ($f_r$):** HWR = $f_{in}$ ; FWR = $2f_{in}$
* **Standard Linear Regulator Dropout:** ~2V (e.g., LM78XX series)
* **LDO Dropout:** < 0.5V (typically 100mV to 300mV)
* **LM317 Internal Reference Voltage ($V_{ref}$):** 1.25V
* **78XX/79XX Series:** 78XX = Positive voltage (e.g., 7805 = +5V); 79XX = Negative voltage (e.g., 7912 = -12V)

**Standards & Protocols:**

* **IEC 62368-1:** International safety standard for IT and Audio/Video equipment power supplies.
* **ATX12V:** Intel standard for desktop computer power supplies.
* **USB Power Delivery (USB-PD):** Protocol for negotiating voltages (5V, 9V, 15V, 20V) over USB Type-C.
* **NEMA Standard:** North American electrical receptacle/plug specifications (e.g., NEMA 1-15, 5-15).

### **2. Comprehension (Concepts & Relationships)**

**Visual Interpretation:**

* **Standard Power Supply Block Diagram:** Transformer $\rightarrow$ Rectifier $\rightarrow$ Filter $\rightarrow$ Regulator $\rightarrow$ Load.
* **HWR Waveform:** Intermittent semi-circles; gaps during reverse bias.
* **FWR Waveform:** Continuous, adjacent semi-circles.
* **Capacitor Input Filter Waveform:** Sawtooth ripple riding on a high DC level.
* **Cockcroft-Walton Multiplier:** Cascaded diode-capacitor ladder; successive nodes provide $V_m$, $2V_m$, $3V_m$, $4V_m$.

**Pros vs. Cons:**

| Power Supply Type | Pros | Cons |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Linear Regulator** | Low noise, simple design, fast transient response | Low efficiency, high heat dissipation, bulky transformers |
| **SMPS (Switching)** | High efficiency, compact, lightweight, wide input range | High EMI/RFI noise, complex design, slower transient response |
| **Bridge FWR** | Lower PIV ($V_m$), smaller transformer utilization factor | Requires 4 diodes, higher voltage drop ($2V_F$) |
| **Center-Tap FWR** | Requires only 2 diodes, lower voltage drop ($V_F$) | High PIV ($2V_m$), requires bulky center-tapped transformer |
| **Voltage Multiplier** | High voltage without expensive step-up transformers | Poor load regulation, low output current capability |

**Exam Traps:**

* **Center-Tap vs. Bridge PIV:** Bridge PIV is **$V_m$**. Center-tap PIV is **$2V_m$**.
* **Ripple Frequency Error:** Using $60\text{Hz}$ instead of $120\text{Hz}$ for Full-Wave filter calculations.
* **Multiplier PIV:** In voltage doublers, triplers, and quadruplers, the PIV rating for **every** diode must be at least **$2V_m$**.
* **Load Regulation Direction:** % Regulation is based on No-Load vs Full-Load. Ideal is 0%.
* **LM317 Resistor Placement:** Mixing up $R_1$ (fixed reference resistor) and $R_2$ (variable adjustment resistor).

**Bottom-Line Summary:**
Power supply and regulation circuits convert volatile, high-voltage AC mains into stable, usable DC power essential for all electronic systems. The transformation relies on sequential stages: magnetic coupling (transformers) for isolation and scaling, non-linear switching (rectifiers) for unidirectional flow, capacitive/inductive networks (filters) to smooth transient ripples, and active feedback loops (linear or switching regulators) to lock the output voltage against line and load variations. Mastery of this domain requires balancing the thermal inefficiencies of clean linear regulators against the high-frequency noise of efficient switching supplies, while precisely calculating diode stress (PIV), ripple factors, and thermal dissipation limits.

### **3. Application (Calculations & Engineering)**

**Formulas:**

* **Average DC Voltage (Unfiltered):**
* HWR: 
$$V_{dc} = \frac{V_m}{\pi} \approx 0.318V_m$$


* FWR: 
$$V_{dc} = \frac{2V_m}{\pi} \approx 0.636V_m$$


* *Where:* $V_m$ = Peak AC secondary voltage (V)


* **RMS Ripple Voltage (Capacitor Filter):**

$$V_{r(rms)} = \frac{I_{dc}}{2\sqrt{3} f_r C}$$


* $I_{dc}$: DC load current (A)
* $f_r$: Ripple frequency (Hz)
* $C$: Filter capacitance (F)


* **Peak-to-Peak Ripple Voltage:**

$$V_{r(p-p)} = \frac{I_{dc}}{f_r C} \approx \frac{V_{dc}}{f_r R_L C}$$


* $R_L$: Load resistance ($\Omega$)


* **Percentage Voltage Regulation (%VR):**

$$\%VR = \frac{V_{NL} - V_{FL}}{V_{FL}} \times 100\%$$


* $V_{NL}$: No-load voltage (V)
* $V_{FL}$: Full-load voltage (V)
* *Meaning:* Measures supply stiffness; lower is better.


* **Linear Regulator Power Dissipation:**

$$P_D = (V_{in} - V_{out}) \cdot I_L + V_{in} \cdot I_Q$$


* $V_{in}, V_{out}$: Input/Output voltages (V)
* $I_L$: Load current (A)
* $I_Q$: Quiescent current (A)
* *Meaning:* Heat generated by dropping voltage across the pass transistor.


* **LM317 Output Voltage:**

$$V_{out} = 1.25\text{V} \cdot \left(1 + \frac{R_2}{R_1}\right) + I_{adj} \cdot R_2$$


* $R_1$: Program resistor (typically $240\Omega$)
* $R_2$: Variable resistor
* $I_{adj}$: Adjustment pin current (often negligible, ~50$\mu$A)



**Practical Use:**

* **Voltage Multipliers:** Photomultiplier tubes (PMTs), CRT displays, bug zappers, ionizers, tasers.
* **Linear Regulators (7805/LM317):** Clean power for sensitive analog front-ends, audio preamplifiers, microcontroller logic (where $V_{in}$ is close to $V_{out}$).
* **SMPS (Buck/Boost/Flyback):** PC power supplies (ATX), laptop chargers, LED drivers, electric vehicle (EV) DC-DC converters.
* **Bleeder Resistors:** Discharging high-voltage filter capacitors for safety when powered off.

**Design Trade-Offs:**

* **Capacitor Size vs. Surge Current:** Larger filter capacitors reduce ripple ($V_{r(p-p)}$) but drastically increase the initial inrush current, potentially blowing rectifiers or fuses.
* **Linear vs. Switching Efficiency:** Dropping 12V to 5V at 1A with a linear regulator dissipates 7W of pure heat (requires massive heat sink). A buck converter does the same with ~90% efficiency (negligible heat).
* **Multiplier Stages vs. Voltage Drop:** Adding stages to a Cockcroft-Walton multiplier increases theoretical voltage, but output impedance heavily limits current, causing massive voltage droop under load.
