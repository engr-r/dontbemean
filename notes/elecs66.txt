### **Topic: Electronic Systems and Design - Security, Safety, and Surveillance Control Systems**

---

### **1. Knowledge (Recall & Facts)**

**Definitions**

* **Access Control System (ACS)**: Hardware/software regulating physical entry (RFID, biometrics, keypads).
* **Intrusion Detection System (IDS)**: Network of sensors (glass-break, magnetic contacts, motion) triggering alarms upon perimeter breach.
* **Passive Infrared (PIR)**: Sensor detecting variations in ambient infrared radiation (body heat) across its field of view.
* **Closed-Circuit Television (CCTV)**: Private video surveillance network.
* **DVR vs. NVR**: **D**igital **V**ideo **R**ecorder (encodes analog signals at the panel) vs. **N**etwork **V**ideo **R**ecorder (receives pre-encoded digital IP streams).
* **Fire Alarm Control Panel (FACP)**: Central hub monitoring **initiating devices** (smoke/heat detectors) and activating **notification appliances** (horns/strobes).
* **Fail-Safe**: Lock releases (opens) upon power loss; strictly prioritized for **life safety** and evacuation.
* **Fail-Secure**: Lock engages (locks) upon power loss; strictly prioritized for **asset protection**.

**Critical Numbers**

* **Human Body IR Emission**: Peak wavelength $\approx 9.4\text{ }\mu\text{m}$ (target for PIR sensors).
* **Standard Video Frequencies**: **$30\text{ fps}$** (NTSC/Real-time), **$24\text{ fps}$** (Cinema/Legacy).
* **PoE (Power over Ethernet) Standard Voltage**: **$48\text{ V DC}$** (IEEE 802.3af/at).
* **Alarm Loop End-of-Line (EOL) Resistor**: Typically **$2.2\text{ k}\Omega$ to $10\text{ k}\Omega$** (supervises wire integrity).
* **Wiegand Protocol Format**: **26-bit** standard (common RFID reader-to-controller data format).
* **CO Detection Threshold**: Alarms trigger at **$70\text{ ppm}$** within 60 to 240 minutes.

**Standards & Protocols**

* **NFPA 72**: National Fire Alarm and Signaling Code (Critical for Safety Officers / SO2 compliance).
* **ONVIF**: Open Network Video Interface Forum (interoperability standard for IP-based physical security products).
* **UL 294**: Standard for Access Control System Units.
* **H.264 / H.265 (HEVC)**: Advanced Video Coding standards defining aggressive video compression.
* **RS-485**: Standard physical layer for long-distance ($1200\text{ m}$) ACS reader/controller networks.

### **2. Comprehension (Concepts & Relationships)**

**Visual Interpretation**

* **FACP Class A vs. Class B Wiring**:
* **Class B**: Linear daisy-chain ending in an EOL resistor. Single wire break disables all downstream devices.
* **Class A**: Redundant return path to the panel. Single wire break still allows full system operation.


* **Camera Field of View (FOV)**: Inversely proportional to focal length. **Short focal length** ($2.8\text{ mm}$) = Wide angle. **Long focal length** ($12\text{ mm}$) = Narrow, zoomed-in telephoto.
* **PIR Fresnel Lens Pattern**: Alternating active/inactive fan-shaped detection zones. Movement *across* the zones triggers an alarm; movement straight *toward* the sensor may fail to trigger.

**Pros vs. Cons**

| Sensor/Tech | Advantages | Disadvantages |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **IP Cameras (IoT)** | High resolution; PoE capability; edge-analytics/AI. | Network bandwidth heavy; vulnerable to cyber attacks. |
| **PIR Sensors** | Extremely cheap; ignores inanimate moving objects. | Prone to false alarms from HVAC vents or direct sunlight. |
| **Dual-Tech (PIR + Microwave)** | Virtual elimination of false alarms (both must trigger). | Expensive; higher power consumption. |
| **Biometric Access** | Highest security (cannot share credentials). | Slower throughput; privacy compliance issues; False Rejection Rate (FRR). |

**Exam Traps**

* **Fail-Safe vs. Fail-Secure Trap**: Fire exits **must** use Fail-Safe locks (Maglocks). High-security IT server racks use Fail-Secure (Electric Strikes).
* **NVR vs. DVR Camera Trap**: NVRs use IP cameras (digital). DVRs use coaxial cameras (analog).
* **Smoke vs. Heat Detector Trap**: Smoke detectors (photoelectric/ionization) for life safety (early warning). Heat detectors (rate-of-rise/fixed) for property protection in dusty/smoky environments (kitchens, garages) where smoke detectors cause false alarms.
* **Wiegand vs. OSDP**: Wiegand is one-way, unencrypted, and legacy. OSDP (Open Supervised Device Protocol) is two-way, encrypted, and modern.

**Bottom-Line Summary**
Security and life-safety systems fuse networked microelectronics, specialized sensors, and rigorous building codes to protect human life and structural assets. Mastery requires distinguishing between life-safety mandates—such as NFPA 72 Class A redundant wiring loops and fail-safe egress protocols—and asset protection architectures leveraging edge-computed IP video and dual-technology intrusion detection. The engineering challenge lies in balancing rigorous false-alarm mitigation through sensor fusion against the massive bandwidth and data-storage demands of high-compression, multi-node IP surveillance arrays.

### **3. Application (Calculations & Engineering)**

**Formulas**

* **Lens Focal Length ($f$)**:
$f = \frac{v \times D}{V}$
* $f$: Focal length ($\text{mm}$)
* $v$: Sensor format height ($\text{mm}$)
* $D$: Distance to subject ($\text{m}$)
* $V$: Vertical field of view required at subject ($\text{m}$)
* *Meaning*: Determines the correct lens to achieve the desired surveillance framing.


* **Video Storage Calculation ($S$)**:
$S = \frac{R \times 86400 \times d \times c}{8 \times 10^9}$
* $S$: Storage required ($\text{GB}$)
* $R$: Bitrate of camera ($\text{bps}$)
* $d$: Number of days to record
* $c$: Number of cameras
* *Meaning*: Calculates hard drive capacity required for an NVR array.


* **System Availability ($A$)**:
$A = \frac{\text{MTBF}}{\text{MTBF} + \text{MTTR}}$
* $A$: Availability (percentage, e.g., $0.99999$)
* $\text{MTBF}$: Mean Time Between Failures ($\text{hours}$)
* $\text{MTTR}$: Mean Time To Repair ($\text{hours}$)
* *Meaning*: Reliability metric for mission-critical safety systems.



**Practical Use**

* **IoT Smart Surveillance**: Utilizing an **ESP32-CAM** coupled with a PIR module to create a low-power, edge-triggered security node that transmits H.264 video to an MQTT broker only when human motion is verified.
* **Fire Evacuation**: FACP directly interfaces with the Access Control System. Upon alarm, FACP cuts power to all door **Maglocks**, reverting them to their fail-safe (open) state for immediate evacuation.
* **Perimeter Defense**: Active Infrared (AIR) photoelectric beams spanning building perimeters, triggering IDS if the beam is broken by physical intrusion.

**Design Trade-Offs**

* **Video Compression (H.264 vs. H.265)**: H.265 uses $\approx 50\%$ less bandwidth/storage than H.264 but requires significantly more processing power (CPU overhead) at the camera and NVR to encode/decode.
* **Motion Detection (Software vs. Hardware)**: Software pixel-based motion detection is free but triggers on shadows/trees. Hardware PIR is immune to shadows but requires physical sensor integration.
* **RFID vs. Biometrics**: RFID offers high throughput (fast clock-ins) but cards can be stolen. Biometrics ensure identity but slow down entry queues during peak shift changes.
