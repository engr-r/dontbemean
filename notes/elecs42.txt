### **1. Knowledge (Recall & Facts)**

**Definitions:**

* **Small Signal:** AC input voltage sufficiently small to maintain linear operation around the DC Q-point.
* **Superposition Theorem:** Fundamental principle for AC analysis; DC sources replaced by ideal internal impedances (voltage sources shorted, current sources opened).
* **Transconductance ($g_m$):** Ratio of AC output current to AC input voltage; relates input control to output drive.
* **$r_e$ Model:** BJT small-signal model utilizing a diode equivalent resistance in the emitter branch.
* **Hybrid-$\pi$ Model:** BJT/FET small-signal model suitable for both low and high-frequency analysis.
* **Early Effect (Base-Width Modulation):** Variation of effective base width with collector-base voltage; modeled as output resistance ($r_o$).
* **Miller Effect:** Multiplication of feedback capacitance/resistance by voltage gain, dominating high-frequency roll-off.
* **Bypass Capacitor:** Capacitor parallel to emitter/source resistor; acts as AC short to maximize voltage gain.
* **Coupling Capacitor:** Blocks DC isolation between stages while passing AC signals.

**Critical Numbers:**

* **Thermal Voltage ($V_T$):** ~26mV (at 300K, room temperature)
* **Standard BJT AC Beta ($\beta$ or $h_{fe}$):** 50 to 400
* **Typical BJT Output Resistance ($r_o$):** 40k$\Omega$ to 100k$\Omega$
* **Early Voltage ($V_A$):** 50V to 200V
* **JFET/MOSFET Input Impedance:** $>10^9\Omega$ (practically $\infty$)
* **Typical FET Transconductance ($g_m$):** 1mS to 10mS
* **Low-Frequency Coupling/Bypass Capacitor Reactance ($X_C$):** Assumed $0\Omega$ (AC Short)

**Standards & Protocols:**

* **IEEE Standard Symbols:** Lowercase letter, lowercase subscript = instantaneous AC (e.g., $i_c$). Uppercase letter, uppercase subscript = DC (e.g., $I_C$). Lowercase letter, uppercase subscript = RMS/Effective AC (e.g., $i_C$).
* **IEEE Std 255-1963:** Letter symbols for semiconductor devices (defines h-parameters: $h_{ie}$, $h_{fe}$, $h_{re}$, $h_{oe}$).
* **JEDEC JESD77:** Standard terms and definitions for discrete semiconductors.

### **2. Comprehension (Concepts & Relationships)**

**Visual Interpretation:**

* **AC Load Line:** Steeper slope than DC load line; intersects DC load line exactly at the Q-point; determines maximum undistorted AC peak-to-peak swing (compliance).
* **BJT $r_e$ vs Hybrid-$\pi$ Diagram:** $r_e$ model places resistance in emitter; Hybrid-$\pi$ places resistance ($r_\pi$) in base. Physically equivalent ($r_\pi = \beta r_e$).
* **FET Small Signal Diagram:** Voltage-controlled current source ($g_m v_{gs}$) at output; open circuit (infinite resistance) at input gate.
* **Waveform Phase Shift:** 180° inversion in Common Emitter (CE) and Common Source (CS) output waveforms relative to input.

**Pros vs. Cons:**

| Configuration | Input Impedance ($Z_{in}$) | Output Impedance ($Z_{out}$) | Voltage Gain ($A_v$) | Current Gain ($A_i$) | Phase Inversion | Primary Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **BJT CE** | Medium (~1k-5k$\Omega$) | Medium (~$R_C$) | High | High | **Yes (180°)** | General purpose amplification |
| **BJT CB** | Very Low (~$r_e$) | High (~$R_C$) | High | $\approx 1$ | No | High-frequency/RF, impedance matching |
| **BJT CC (Emitter Follower)** | High (~$\beta R_E$) | Very Low (~$r_e$) | $\approx 1$ | High | No | Voltage buffer, driving low-$Z$ loads |
| **FET CS** | Very High ($\infty$) | Medium (~$R_D$) | Medium | High | **Yes (180°)** | Voltage amplifier, low noise |
| **FET CG** | Low (~$1/g_m$) | High (~$R_D$) | Medium | $\approx 1$ | No | High-frequency RF amplifiers |
| **FET CD (Source Follower)** | Very High ($\infty$) | Low (~$1/g_m$) | $\approx 1$ | High | No | Impedance matching, buffers |

**Exam Traps:**

* **DC vs AC Alpha/Beta:** Confusing $\beta_{DC}$ ($h_{FE}$) with $\beta_{ac}$ ($h_{fe}$). Often assumed equal in basic problems, but technically distinct.
* **Ignoring $r_o$ / $h_{oe}$:** Neglecting output resistance when an Early Voltage ($V_A$) or admittance parameter ($h_{oe}$) is explicitly provided.
* **Emitter/Source Resistor Status:** Missing whether $R_E$ or $R_S$ is bypassed by a capacitor. If bypassed, exclude from AC model. If unbypassed, it drastically reduces gain (swamping/degeneration).
* **BJT vs FET Control:** BJT is **current-controlled** ($i_c = \beta i_b$). FET is **voltage-controlled** ($i_d = g_m v_{gs}$). Do not mix equations.

**Bottom-Line Summary:**
Small-signal analysis translates complex, non-linear semiconductor devices into simplified, linear AC equivalent circuits by isolating the minute alternating currents fluctuating around a strictly established DC quiescent point. By systematically neutralizing all DC sources and deploying standardized models—such as the BJT $r_e$ or Hybrid-$\pi$ model and the FET transconductance model—engineers can rapidly calculate critical amplifier parameters including input impedance, output impedance, and voltage gain. Mastery of these linear models is non-negotiable for board exams, as it forms the predictive foundation for all discrete analog amplifier design, impedance matching networks, and frequency response profiling.

### **3. Application (Calculations & Engineering)**

**Formulas:**

* **BJT Emitter Resistance ($r_e$):**

$$r_e = \frac{V_T}{I_E} \approx \frac{26\text{mV}}{I_E}$$


* $V_T$: Thermal voltage (V)
* $I_E$: DC Emitter current (A)
* *Meaning:* Dynamic AC resistance of the forward-biased base-emitter junction.


* **BJT Transconductance ($g_m$):**

$$g_m = \frac{I_C}{V_T} = \frac{1}{r_e}$$


* $I_C$: DC Collector current (A)
* *Meaning:* Voltage-to-current conversion efficiency of the BJT.


* **BJT Input Resistance (Hybrid-$\pi$):**

$$r_\pi = \beta r_e = \frac{\beta}{g_m}$$


* $\beta$: AC current gain
* *Meaning:* AC resistance seen looking directly into the base terminal.


* **BJT Output Resistance ($r_o$):**

$$r_o = \frac{V_A + V_{CEQ}}{I_C} \approx \frac{V_A}{I_C}$$


* $V_A$: Early Voltage (V)
* $V_{CEQ}$: DC Collector-Emitter voltage (V)
* *Meaning:* Shunt output resistance due to base-width modulation.


* **JFET / Depletion-MOSFET Transconductance ($g_m$):**

$$g_m = g_{m0} \left(1 - \frac{V_{GS}}{V_P}\right) = \frac{2I_{DSS}}{|V_P|} \left(1 - \frac{V_{GS}}{V_P}\right)$$


* $I_{DSS}$: Drain current at zero gate voltage (A)
* $V_P$: Pinch-off voltage (V)
* $V_{GS}$: DC Gate-Source voltage (V)
* *Meaning:* Slope of the transfer characteristic curve at the Q-point.


* **Enhancement-MOSFET Transconductance ($g_m$):**

$$g_m = 2k(V_{GS} - V_{TH})$$


* $k$: Device conduction parameter ($\text{A/V}^2$)
* $V_{TH}$: Threshold voltage (V)
* *Meaning:* Gain parameter derived from physical MOSFET dimensions and bias.


* **CE/CS Unbypassed Voltage Gain (Approximate):**

$$A_v = -\frac{R_{L\_AC}}{r_e + R_E} \quad \text{(BJT)} \quad | \quad A_v = -\frac{g_m R_{L\_AC}}{1 + g_m R_S} \quad \text{(FET)}$$


* $R_{L\_AC}$: Total AC load resistance ($R_C \parallel R_L$ or $R_D \parallel R_L$)
* *Meaning:* Gain significantly reduced (swamped) but highly stable against temperature/$\beta$ variations.



**Practical Use:**

* **BJT CE / FET CS:** First-stage audio pre-amplifiers, low-noise amplification blocks.
* **Emitter Follower (CC):** Output stages of audio power amplifiers (Class AB push-pull) to drive 8$\Omega$ speakers.
* **Cascode Amplifier (CE-CB cascade):** Wideband oscilloscopes and RF front-ends (nullifies Miller capacitance).
* **FET Input Stages:** Digital multimeters, electrometer probes, oscilloscope active probes (prevents circuit loading).

**Design Trade-Offs:**

* **Gain vs. Stability (Emitter Degeneration):** Leaving $R_E$ unbypassed drastically reduces voltage gain but makes the gain independent of highly volatile BJT $\beta$ values and temperature fluctuations.
* **Input Impedance vs. Noise:** FETs offer near-infinite input impedance (no loading) but typically suffer from higher 1/f (flicker) noise compared to BJTs at low frequencies.
* **Output Impedance vs. Power Efficiency:** Lowering collector resistor ($R_C$) improves output drive capability (lower $Z_{out}$) but reduces overall voltage gain and increases quiescent power dissipation.

**High-Yield Exam Triggers:**

| Keyword / Visual Cue | Memory Shortcut / Translation | Typical Exam Question Format |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **"Swamped" Amplifier** | $R_E$ is strictly UNBYPASSED. | Calculate $A_v$; Expect $A_v \approx R_C/R_E$. |
| **$V_A = \infty$ or $h_{oe} = 0$** | Ideal output. $r_o = \infty$ (open circuit). | Calculate $Z_{out}$ or $A_v$ ignoring the device's internal output resistance. |
| **"Bootstrapping"** | Feedback technique to increase $Z_{in}$. | Identify circuit topology; calculate the newly elevated input impedance. |
| **JFET Transfer Curve given** | Find slope at Q-point for $g_m$. | Determine transconductance graphically from $\Delta I_D / \Delta V_{GS}$. |
| **"Cascaded Stages"** | Total $A_v = A_{v1} \cdot A_{v2} \cdot A_{v3}...$ | Calculate overall gain in dB ($A_{v(dB)} = A_{v1(dB)} + A_{v2(dB)}...$). *Crucial: $Z_{in}$ of stage 2 becomes load of stage 1.* |
