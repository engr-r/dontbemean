### **1. Knowledge (Recall & Facts)**

**Definitions:**

* **Time-Varying Fields:** Electric and magnetic fields dynamically changing with time, mutually inducing one another.
* **Faraday’s Law of Induction:** A time-varying magnetic field induces an electromotive force (EMF) and a circulating electric field.
* **Lenz’s Law:** Induced EMF generates a current whose magnetic field **opposes** the original change in magnetic flux.
* **Transformer EMF:** EMF induced by a time-varying magnetic field in a **stationary** loop.
* **Motional EMF:** EMF induced by a **moving** loop in a static magnetic field.
* **Displacement Current ($\vec{J}_d$):** Maxwell’s pivotal addition to Ampere’s Law; represents the rate of change of electric flux density.
* **Time-Harmonic Fields:** Fields varying sinusoidally with time, analyzed using steady-state **phasors**.
* **Retarded Potentials:** Time delay in electromagnetic effects at a distance due to the finite speed of light.
* **Maxwell’s Equations:** Four fundamental equations (Faraday, Ampere-Maxwell, Gauss-Electric, Gauss-Magnetic) governing all macroscopic electromagnetism.
* **Continuity Equation:** Mathematical statement of the **conservation of charge**.

**Critical Numbers:**

* **Speed of Light ($c$):** $3 \times 10^8$ m/s
* **Free Space Permittivity ($\epsilon_0$):** $8.854 \times 10^{-12}$ F/m
* **Free Space Permeability ($\mu_0$):** $4\pi \times 10^{-7}$ H/m
* **Intrinsic Impedance of Free Space ($\eta_0$):** $377\ \Omega$ (or $120\pi\ \Omega$)
* **Divergence of Magnetic Flux ($\nabla \cdot \vec{B}$):** Exactly **0** (No magnetic monopoles).

**Standards & Protocols:**

* **FCC Part 15:** US regulations for unintentional and intentional radiated electromagnetic emissions (EMI/EMC).
* **CISPR 11 / EN 55011:** Industrial, scientific, and medical (ISM) radio-frequency disturbance characteristics.
* **IEEE C95.1:** Safety standard for human exposure to time-varying electromagnetic fields ($0\text{ Hz}$ to $300\text{ GHz}$).

### **2. Comprehension (Concepts & Relationships)**

**Visual Interpretation:**

* **Maxwell’s Curl Equations:** Time-varying $\vec{B}$ creates closed loops of $\vec{E}$ (Faraday). Time-varying $\vec{E}$ creates closed loops of $\vec{H}$ (Ampere-Maxwell). **Orthogonal, self-sustaining chain reaction**.
* **Displacement Current in a Capacitor:** Conduction current stops at the plates; displacement current flows *through* the dielectric, maintaining continuous total current.
* **Wave Propagation Direction:** Pointing vector ($\vec{E} \times \vec{H}$) specifies power flow orthogonal to both field vectors.

**Pros vs. Cons (Current Types in Time-Varying Fields):**

| Current Type | Pros / Characteristics | Cons / Limitations |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Conduction Current ($\vec{J}_c$)** | Physical movement of free electrons; drives standard circuitry. | Causes $I^2R$ (Joule heating) power losses; cannot cross dielectrics. |
| **Displacement Current ($\vec{J}_d$)** | Allows AC current "flow" through capacitors; enables EM wave propagation in vacuums. | Carries zero real power (purely reactive); zero physical charge transfer. |
| **Eddy Currents** | Useful for induction heating and magnetic braking. | Major source of core loss in AC transformers and motors. |

**Exam Traps:**

* **Missing the Minus Sign:** Forgetting Lenz's Law ($-d\Phi/dt$) leads to inverted polarities in EMF calculations.
* **Static Ampere’s Law Application:** Using $\nabla \times \vec{H} = \vec{J}$ for AC circuits (omitting $\partial \vec{D} / \partial t$) causes massive errors at high frequencies.
* **Capacitor DC vs. AC:** Assuming current through a capacitor is always zero. (DC = true, AC = false due to displacement current).
* **Phasor Time Derivative:** Forgetting that taking the time derivative ($\partial/\partial t$) in the time domain equates to multiplying by **$j\omega$** in the phasor domain.
* **Moving vs. Stationary Loop:** Applying transformer EMF formula to a moving coil problem (must use $\vec{v} \times \vec{B}$ for motional EMF).

**Bottom-Line Summary:**
Time-varying electromagnetic fields bridge the isolated realms of electrostatics and magnetostatics, formalized by Maxwell's Equations, where a changing magnetic field induces a circulating electric field (Faraday's Law) and a time-varying electric field generates a circulating magnetic field via displacement current (Ampere-Maxwell Law). This absolute mathematical symmetry dictates the existence of self-propagating electromagnetic waves—freeing energy from physical conductors—and establishes the foundational framework for all high-frequency AC circuit design, antenna radiation theory, and modern wireless communications.

### **3. Application (Calculations & Engineering)**

**Formulas:**

* **Faraday’s Law (Integral Form):**

$$V_{emf} = \oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l} = -\frac{d}{dt} \int_S \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{S}$$


* $V_{emf}$: Electromotive Force (V); $\vec{B}$: Magnetic Flux Density (T).
* *Meaning:* Voltage induced equals the negative rate of change of magnetic flux.


* **Motional EMF:**

$$V_{emf} = \oint (\vec{v} \times \vec{B}) \cdot d\vec{l}$$


* $\vec{v}$: Velocity vector (m/s).
* *Meaning:* Voltage generated by a conductor cutting through magnetic field lines.


* **Displacement Current Density ($\vec{J}_d$):**

$$\vec{J}_d = \frac{\partial \vec{D}}{\partial t}$$


* $\vec{J}_d$: Current density (A/m$^2$); $\vec{D}$: Electric Flux Density (C/m$^2$).


* **Maxwell’s Equations (Differential / Point Form - Time Domain):**
1. **Faraday:** $\nabla \times \vec{E} = -\frac{\partial \vec{B}}{\partial t}$
2. **Ampere-Maxwell:** $\nabla \times \vec{H} = \vec{J}_c + \frac{\partial \vec{D}}{\partial t}$
3. **Gauss (Electric):** $\nabla \cdot \vec{D} = \rho_v$
4. **Gauss (Magnetic):** $\nabla \cdot \vec{B} = 0$


* **Maxwell’s Equations (Differential / Point Form - Phasor Domain):**
1. $\nabla \times \vec{E}_s = -j\omega\mu\vec{H}_s$
2. $\nabla \times \vec{H}_s = (\sigma + j\omega\epsilon)\vec{E}_s$
3. $\nabla \cdot \vec{D}_s = \rho_{vs}$
4. $\nabla \cdot \vec{B}_s = 0$


* **Continuity of Current Equation:**

$$\nabla \cdot \vec{J} = -\frac{\partial \rho_v}{\partial t}$$


* $\vec{J}$: Volume current density; $\rho_v$: Volume charge density.
* *Meaning:* Current diverging from a point equals the rate of charge depletion at that point.



**Practical Use:**

* **Transformers:** Utilizing transformer EMF to step-up/step-down AC grid voltages.
* **Electric Generators:** Utilizing motional EMF to convert mechanical turbine rotation into grid AC power.
* **RFID & Inductive Charging:** Near-field magnetic coupling utilizing Faraday's law at high frequencies.
* **Antennas:** Utilizing time-varying conduction currents to launch self-sustaining displacement currents (EM waves) into free space.

**Design Trade-Offs:**

* **Frequency vs. Radiation Leakage:** Increasing operational frequency ($\omega$) allows for smaller inductors/capacitors and faster data rates, but mathematically amplifies the displacement current ($\partial \vec{D}/\partial t$), turning standard PCB traces into unintended radiating antennas (EMI issues).
* **Transformer Core vs. Eddy Currents:** Time-varying magnetic fields induce circulating currents inside the transformer core itself (Faraday's Law). Solid cores suffer massive heat loss; laminated cores disrupt the $d\vec{l}$ path, minimizing loss but increasing manufacturing cost.
* **Conductor Size vs. Skin Effect:** At high frequencies, time-varying internal magnetic fields force conduction current entirely to the conductor's surface. Thicker wires do not lower AC resistance; designers must use expensive Litz wire or hollow tubing.
